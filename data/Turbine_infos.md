# Wind Turbine Technical Specifications – GERAR 246 (Enersud)

## Overview

This document describes the technical specifications of the wind turbine model GERAR 246 manufactured by Enersud. This turbine is designed for small-scale wind power generation and is widely used in experimental and distributed energy systems.

The wind power data used in this project were generated based on wind speed measurements and the operational characteristics of this turbine model.

---

## General Characteristics

Manufacturer: Enersud
Model: GERAR 246
Type: Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine (HAWT)
Number of Blades: 3

Rotor Diameter: **2.46 m**

Total Weight: **35 kg**

---

## Power and Performance

Rated Power: **1000 W at 12.5 m/s**

Cut-in Wind Speed: **2.0 m/s**

The turbine is designed to operate efficiently at low wind speeds, enabling energy generation in moderate wind regimes.

---

## Aerodynamic Characteristics

Blade Type: Twisted blades with **5 airfoils**

Control Strategy: **Active Stall**

High Wind Protection:

* **Synchronized pitch control system** (patented by Enersud)

This system allows controlled operation under increasing wind speeds while maintaining safety and reducing noise.

---

## Electrical System

Generator Type: Permanent Magnet Generator (Neodymium)

Topology: **Axial Flux Generator**

Electrical Configuration: **Three-phase AC**

Output Voltage Options:

* 12 V
* 24 V
* 48 V

---

## Mechanical and Material Characteristics

Anti-corrosion Materials:

* Aluminum
* Stainless steel
* Galvanized components

The turbine is designed for durability under harsh environmental conditions such as wind, rain, and marine environments.

---

## Control and Operation

The turbine includes:

* Charge controller for battery systems
* Dump load system for excess energy dissipation
* Electromagnetic braking capability

It can operate in:

* Off-grid systems (battery storage)
* Grid-connected systems (with appropriate inverter)

---

## Operating Behavior

* The turbine starts rotating at low wind speeds (~2 m/s)
* Power output increases nonlinearly with wind speed
* At higher wind speeds, pitch control limits rotational speed and maintains safe operation

---

## Notes

* The name "246" refers to the rotor diameter (2.46 m)
* The turbine is classified as a **microgeneration wind system**
* The dataset used in this research relies on simulated power output derived from wind speed measurements using this turbine's characteristics
